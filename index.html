The Subscapularis Muscle!

What is the Subscapularis muscle?

The Subscapularis muscle is one of the rotator cuff muscles, the rotator cuff muscles help stabilize the shoulder joint. The rotator cuff is made up of four muscles including...

  • Supraspinatus
  • Infraspinatus
  • Teres minor
  • Subscapularis

Actions of rotator muscles include all the possible motions of the shoulder...

  • Flexion
  • Extension
  • Abduction
  • Adduction
  • Internal Rotation
  • External Rotation

Origin

Subscapular fossa of scapula

Insertion

Lesser tubercle of humerus

Action

Internally rotates the shoulder and to stabilize the head of the humerus

Innervation

C5-C6, upper and lower subscapular nerves

Common injuries to the rotator cuff muscles

Tendonitis

Inflammation of a tendon, common places include the elbows, knee's, and achilles

Bursitis

Inflammation of the bursa sacs which is a structure in joints used to reduce friction

Rotator Cuff Tears

A tear in one of the rotator cuff muscles. The tendon is either partially torn or completely torn.

How to treat shoulder injuries

Tendonitis

  • Reduce pain and inflammation
  • Gradually increase ROM
  • iceing and rest
  • Physical Therapy to strengthen and to restore function
  • Medication such as ibuprofen or cortisone injections
  • If conservative options do not work, then surgery may be needed

Bursitis

  • Rest and stop activities that cause pain
  • NSAIDS such as ibuprofen
  • Physical therapy to strengthen muscles and to improve shoulder function
  • Corticosteroid injections
  • If conservative options fail then surgery may be needed

Rotator Cuff Tears

  • Depends on how bad the tear is
  • Partial tears can heal on their own and can improve without surgery
  • Wearing a sling and resting the shoulder
  • NSAIDS such as ibuprofen
  • Physical therapy to maintain and restore function
  • Corticosteroid injections
  • If conservative options do not work then surgery is required to fix the damaged joint
